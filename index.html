About Football

Association football, or soccer, is played between two teams of 11 players on a rectangular field with goals at each end. The objective is to score by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal, primarily using any part of the body except the hands and arms, with only goalkeepers permitted to handle the ball within their penalty area.

The sport is governed by the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) and is the world's most popular sport, with an estimated 250 million players in over 200 countries.

American football is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players on a rectangular field with goalposts at each end. The offense attempts to advance the ball down the field by running or passing, aiming to score points by reaching the opponent's end zone or kicking the ball through the goalposts.

The National Football League (NFL) is the premier professional league in the United States, with the Super Bowl being one of the most-watched sporting events globally.

The term "football" can refer to different sports depending on the region. In the United States and Canada, "football" typically refers to American football, while in most other parts of the world, it denotes association football (soccer).

Other variations include Australian rules football, Canadian football, and Gaelic football, each with unique rules and cultural significance.
